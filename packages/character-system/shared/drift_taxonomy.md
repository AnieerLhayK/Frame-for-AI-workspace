# Drift Taxonomy

This taxonomy gives the shared vocabulary for diagnosing character skill failures.

## AI Smell

Output sounds like a default assistant response: balanced, helpful, polished, and abstract, but without the target voice's pressure, risk, or specificity.

Signals:

- neutral explanatory phrasing
- generic transitions
- excessive symmetry
- safe concluding summaries
- no local texture

## Generic Writing

Output could belong to any writer or assistant. It may be correct but not distinctive.

Signals:

- interchangeable metaphors
- stock emotional language
- broad claims without observed detail
- predictable sentence shapes

## Style Collapse

The skill loses the target style under task pressure and falls back to a simpler pattern.

Signals:

- rhythm flattens
- references stop mattering
- anti-patterns reappear
- output follows the user's instruction literally but loses voice

## Emotion Too Direct

The text names feelings before earning them through scene, rhythm, contrast, or implication.

Signals:

- "I felt sad", "it was lonely", "this was beautiful"
- moral or emotional explanation replaces lived detail
- no contradiction or restraint

## Rhythm Error

The sentence and paragraph movement no longer matches the character's cadence.

Signals:

- all sentences have similar length
- pauses feel mechanical
- long sentences ramble without pressure
- short sentences land as slogans

## Ending Too Summarized

The ending explains what the piece means instead of leaving a precise afterimage or unresolved pressure.

Signals:

- explicit takeaway
- broad final lesson
- final sentence repeats the theme
- reflective closure arrives too neatly

## Imagery Stacking

Images accumulate without hierarchy or narrative function.

Signals:

- too many image systems in one passage
- decorative detail replaces movement
- metaphors compete instead of deepening

## Fake Literary Tone

The prose performs seriousness without concrete necessity.

Signals:

- ornate phrasing without tension
- abstract nouns carrying the emotion
- pseudo-profound contrast
- atmospheric language detached from task
